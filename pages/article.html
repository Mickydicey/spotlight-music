The Voice From Enugu You Need To Hear Right Now

In a small studio in Enugu, 22-year-old Chinemerem is quietly making some of the most soulful Afrobeats you've never heard.

S
SpotLight Editorial Published Nov 15, 2024 · 6 min read

The first time I heard Chinemerem, I stopped what I was doing. It was 2am, I was scrolling through Instagram, and someone had posted a 30-second clip of a song called "Adanna Rising." I played it back seven times.

That's the thing about really good music — you know it in your gut before your brain catches up. And Chinemerem's music has that effect. Soulful, patient, unhurried. In a Naija music landscape obsessed with the next viral hit, he's making songs that feel like they've always existed.

I called him a week later.

The Sound of Home

Chinemerem Okafor is 22 years old. He records in a converted bedroom in his family's home in Enugu. His microphone is second-hand. His interface is entry-level. His talent is not.

"I don't have a plan," he tells me over a spotty video call. "I just have songs."

He has more than songs. He has a philosophy. His debut EP, Adanna, named after his grandmother, is a five-track meditation on Eastern Nigerian identity, family, and finding your voice when nobody's listening.

Igbo Melodies, Modern Production

What sets Chinemerem apart isn't just his voice — though that voice is a rare instrument, warm and worn like an old jazz recording. It's what he does with tradition.

"I grew up in church," he says. "But my grandmother sang the old Igbo songs. Highlife. Osadebe. Oliver De Coque. I couldn't help but absorb it."

"I'm not trying to be the next anyone. I'm trying to make music my grandmother would be proud of. If it moves someone, that's enough."

— Chinemerem

Not Waiting To Be Signed

What's remarkable about artists like Chinemerem is that they're not waiting for permission anymore. There's no label meeting in his future. No A&R showcase. No agent chasing streams.

"I put songs out because they're finished," he shrugs. "If someone hears them, that's a bonus."

What Comes Next

Chinemerem is working on his second EP. He won't say when it drops. "When it's ready," he laughs.

You should be part of it. Start with "Adanna Rising." Then go deeper.

Remember his name.
